WHAT AM I FROM?

“ I Am From”

I am from the wonderful furniture

From ranger scout spray and coal

I am from the natural kitchen on the side of my house, fresh, full of air

I am from the abandon pinky rose apple

The sourest smoothest mango whose long gone limbs I remember as if they were my own.

I’m from Chinese new year, Khmer new year and an empathetic person

From my mom and my dad

I’m from waking up in the early morning and sweeping the floor every day

I’m from the ability to understand lesson faster and at the same time forget it faster and

from who afraid teachers and rules the most.

I’m from going to pagoda when Pchum Ben festival

Accompany by many food and snacks

And listen to the monk pray but not much.

I’m from Kampot and the slender river where there are many boats

Fried shrimp with dried red bell pepper and Steam Crabs

From my great grandfather who I never met before in my life

who died before I was born since Khmer Rouge and left my great grandma alone

The first picture necklace of them I’ve ever seen before

On my grandma’s neck that she’s wearing every day and will never forget about this
memento.

By Naka Date: 1/16/2019
